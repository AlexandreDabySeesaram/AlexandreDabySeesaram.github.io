Personal information

He is a Ph.D. candidate in computational mechanics at Université Paris-Saclay (ENS Paris-Saclay) in the LMPS (Laboratory of Mechanics Paris-Saclay). His main research interest consists of solving highly nonlinear dynamics problems using model order reduction techniques such as the Proper Generalised Decomposition (PGD) coupled with the LATIN method.

Publications

Recent Work

Nonlinear Dynamics with PGD

A hybrid frequency-temporal reduced-order method for nonlinear dynamics. The solver is based on the LATIN-PGD method where a reduced-order model method called the Proper Generalised Decomposition (PGD) is employed.

Transient dynamics with FFT

The FFT algorithm is used even in transient dynamics or with residual displacements due to plasticity by introducing artificial damping in order to avoid Gibbs phenomena

Multi-query strategy

Enhanced reduced-order model for efficient multi-load computations of nonlinear dynamics problems.
